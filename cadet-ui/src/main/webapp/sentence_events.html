In this task you are being asked to verify the results of an automatic text processing tool. In technical terms, the goal of our tool is to identify what we will call complex predicates, and their arguments. More loosely, we are concerned with finding individual statements that are contained within larger sentences.

Consider the following sentence:

John , the manager of Acme , bought a car .

This sentence has various statements contained within it, such as:

John is/are the manager of Acme

John bought a car

We want to be able to automatically identify statements such as those, but sometimes the underlying tools make mistakes. For example, the following example would be judged as incorrect, based on that same initial sentence:

John bought

That is an example of a statement that is missing an argument. The following are examples where the bracketing is wrong: certain words are missing or incorrectly included in the statement:

John, the manager bought a car

the manager of Acme bought a car

Please note that we are not concerned with whether an extracted statement is true or supported by the initial sentence. For example:

If John bought a car then Mary would too .

We would like to extract the statement:

John bought a car

Similarly, in the sentence:

Mary wished that John bought a car .

We would again like to extract the statement:

John bought a car

Some statements that we present contain text that is partially generated, for example:

John 's car is red .

Would lead to the statement:

John has/have car

This is a correct statement. It is not a grammatical sentence, but it does capture something correctly from the original sentence: John has a car. Since the original sentence contained only car and not a car, then the automatically generated statement also does not contain a car. This is not an error, we would want you to judge this as correct. The purpose of the task is to verify that we are highlighting the predicates and the arguments as best as possible, not leaving things out that are there in the initial sentence.
